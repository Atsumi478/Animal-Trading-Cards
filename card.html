Okinawa rail

The Okinawa rail, or “Yanbaru Kuina” in Japanese, is the rarest of all and was registered as a Natural Monument of Japan.

  • Avrage size: Midium (30~50 cm)
  • Habitat: Subtropical moist forests
  • Taxonomy: Northern Okinawa
  • Conservation status: Endangered

Throughout the world, this wild bird only lives in the Yanbaru area of northern Okinawa and is unlikely to show itself to humans due to its cautious personality. When driving in northern Okinawa, there are often road signs with a picture of the Okinawa rail. Please drive carefully if you come across a warning sign for them jumping out!
